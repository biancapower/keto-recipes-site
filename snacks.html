Jalapeño Popper Egg Cups

Approx. 45 minutes to make.

Ingredients

  • 12 slices bacon
  • 10 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup shredded Cheddar
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella
  • 2 jalapeños, 1 minced and 1 thinly sliced
  • 1 tsp. garlic powder
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Nonstick cooking spray

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook bacon until slightly browned but still pliable. Set aside on a paper towel-lined plate to drain.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, sour cream, cheeses, minced jalapeño and garlic powder. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Using nonstick cooking spray, grease a muffin tin. Line each well with one slice of bacon, then pour egg mixture into each muffin cup until about two-thirds of the way to the top. Top each muffin with a jalapeño slice.
  4. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the eggs no longer look wet. Cool slightly before removing from the muffin tin.

Stuffed Mini Bell Peppers

Approx. 10 minutes to make.

Ingredients

  • 8 mini bell peppers
  • 225g cream cheese
  • 30g air-dried chorizo, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 tbsp mild chipotle paste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme

Instructions

  1. Split the bell peppers lengthwise and remove the core.
  2. Chop the sausage and herbs finely.
  3. Mix cheese, spices and oil in a small bowl. Add sausage and herbs, and stir together.
  4. Spread out the cheese cream into the bell peppers and serve as a snack or appetizer.

Eggplant Fries

Approx. 25 minutes to make.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggplants
  • 475ml almond flour
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil spray

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Peel eggplant and cut into french-fry shapes. Set aside.
  3. In a shallow bowl, stir together the almond flour, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Place the eggs in a separate bowl and whisk until frothy.
  4. Dip the eggplant pieces into the egg, then into the flour mixture, then back into the egg, and back into the flour mixture.
  5. Place the coated fries on a greased cookie sheet, and drizzle with melted coconut oil.
  6. Bake for 15 minutes or until crispy and brown.
